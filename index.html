294 schools lie above Herd Immunity

Across the 778 elementary schools in Oklahoma with vaccination records or exemption records on File only 294 have a vaccination rate high enough for herd immunity: the vaccination rate needed to protect those who are immunocompromised from diseases such as measles, mumps, and rubella. This includes only 33 percent of private schools in the state. Oklahoma City school district, one of the largest school districts in the state has 20 of its 54 elementary schools within herd immunity range and Tulsa (second largest) only has 7 of its 50 Elementary schools at 95 percent or higher.

392 schools lie within the 80-95% range. This leaves roughly 27,038 students below the national average of 94%. These schools comprise 51 percent of all students in the state and 45 percent of all private school students.

The recent measles outbreak in New York and the increase in whooping cough within Oklahoma in 2016 were reported to take place within tight knit communities. A similar pattern can be seen within the schools that fall below the 80 percent vaccination rate. The median class size doe the 80 percent and below range is around 38.5 students compared to the the state median of 55 students. Often lack of vaccination is due to exemption, Oklahoma allows for medical, religious, or personal exemptions according to the Oklahoma Health Department, but at least 49 schools below the 80% mark had zero exemptions.

** Up to date for all vaccines is defined as 4 or 5 doses of DTaP, 3 or 4 doses of Polio, 2 or more doses of MMR, 3 or more doses of Hep B, 2 or more doses of Hep A, and 1 or more doses of Varicella administered according to recommended age and intervals.

***Exemption defined as documentation on file for a vaccine exemption to one or more antigens. There are three different types of exemptions: medical, religious and personal.

815 public, private, and charter schools in Oklahoma completed the kindergarten survey in 2017-18. Schools that did not complete the survey are not listed.
